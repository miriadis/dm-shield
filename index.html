Falling damage

1d6 bludgeoning damage for every 10ft fallen (max 20d6), lands prone, unless it avoids taking damage from the fall.

Suffocating

A creature can hold its breath for 1 + its CON modifier minutes (minimum 30s).

When out of breath or choking, it can survive for its CON modifier rounds (minimum 1). It can't be stabilized or regain hit points until it can breathe again.

Food & Water

A creature can go 3 + CON days (minimum 1) without food. After that, they suffer 1 level of exhaustion per day.

Drinking less than necessary requires a DC15 CON saving throw. On a fail, suffer 1 level of exhaustion.

Grapple

Spend an Attack action to grapple a creature.

Premise. Grapple a creature up to one size larger than you, within your reach.

Check. Athletics versus Athletics or Acrobatics.

Grappled. Target's speed becomes 0, it can't benefit from any bonus to its speed, and has disadvantage on attack rolls.

Escaping. Target spends its action to roll the check again.

Dragging. You can move with the grappled creature at half speed, or at normal speed if it's two or more sizes smaller than you.

Criticals. When the grappler attacks the grappled creature, 18 on the dice is considered a critical hit.

Disarming Strike

You can use a weapon attack to knock a weapon or another item from the target's grasp.

Contest. Attack roll versus Athletics or Acrobatics.

Two-handed. You have disadvantage on the attack roll if the target is holding the item with two or more hands.

Size. The target has disadvantage on the check if it is smaller than the attacker, or advantage if it is larger.

Passing through

Overrun. Athletics versus Athletics to move through an enemy's space with an action or bonus action. On a failure, doesn't move and gets knocked prone. Advantage if bigger than the target.

Tumble. Acrobatics versus Athletics to move through an enemy's space. On a failure, doesn't move and gets knocked prone. Advantage if smaller than the target.

Jumping

You can cover the full distance if you move at least 10ft immediately before the jump, or half that without moving. Jumping costs movement as usual.

Long Jump. STR score × feet.

Landing. DC10 Acrobatics to land on difficult terrain, or fall prone.

High Jump. STR modifier + 3ft.

Reach. You can reach above you a distance equal to the height of the jump + 1 ½ times your height with your arms extended.

Underwater Combat

Melee Weapon Attack. Disadvantage on attack rolls for creatures without a swimming speed unless attacking with a dagger, javelin, shortsword, spear or trident.

Ranged Weapon Attack. Disadvantage on attack rolls unless attacking with a crossbow, a net, a javelin, a spear, a trident, a dart, or similar thrown weapons. Automatic miss beyond normal range.

Fire Resistance. When fully immersed in water, creatures and objects are resistant to fire damage.

Squeezing

Fitting. A creature can squeeze into a space one size smaller than it.

Moving. A creature moves at half speed through smaller spaces.

Penalties. Disadvantage on attack rolls and DEX saving throws, and hostile attack rolls have advantage.

Mounted Combat

Premise. A creature can mount another creature that is at least one size larger than it.

Mounting & Dismounting. Spend half of your speed to mount or dismount a creature within 5ft.

Controlled Mount. A trained mount's initiative matches yours, and can only Dash, Disengage or Dodge.

Independent Mount. An independent mount maintains its initiative, and can act and move as it wishes.

Falling. If the mount is moved against its will, or you are knocked prone, DC10 DEX saving throw or fall off, landing prone beside it. If the mount is knocked prone, you can spend your reaction to dismount and land safely or fall prone beside it.

Targeting. If the mount provokes an opportunity attack, the attacker can target you or the mount.

Shove

Spend an Attack action to shove a creature.

Straight. Athletics versus Athletics or Acrobatics to knock a target (up to one size larger than you) prone or push it 5ft away with an action.

Aside. Use shove with disadvantage to move the target to a space within reach.

Getting up. If the shover is within reach, the target has no reaction until its next turn, and has disadvantage on attack rolls. The shover can also use its reaction to try shoving prone the target again.

Concentration

Casting. You lose concentration if you cast another spell that requires concentration.

Taking damage. DC10 or half the damage taken (choose the higher) CON saving throw to maintain concentration.

Summoning random creatures

For each summoned creature or as a group, the player can make a spellcasting ability check to either choose what is summoned or narrow down the possible outcome.

Choose creatures. DC16 to choose creatures specifically.

Physical feature. DC13 to randomly summon creatures of one type, size, alignment, armor class, hit points average, speed or ability score.

On a failure, creatures are summoned randomly.
